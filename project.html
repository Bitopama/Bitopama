Road Traffic Dashboard

Goal

The primary goal of this project was to analyze road traffic accident data to uncover key trends and insights about accident severity, vehicle involvement, road conditions, and external factors such as weather. The objective was to provide a clear, interactive overview of the data through a dashboard, enabling stakeholders to better understand accident dynamics and make data-driven decisions to enhance road safety.

  • View on GitHub

Background Information

The dataset provided for this analysis contained over 300,000 records of road traffic accidents, including detailed information on accident severity (fatal, serious, slight), types of vehicles involved, weather conditions, road surface details, and accident locations (urban vs. rural). Before the data could be effectively analyzed, it underwent a rigorous cleaning process to remove duplicates and resolve inconsistencies. This step was essential to ensure the reliability and accuracy of any insights derived from the data. The cleaned dataset then served as the foundation for a comprehensive traffic accident analysis.

Dashboard Design and Creation

After cleaning the dataset, I utilized DAX to create measures that allowed me to dynamically calculate the total number of accidents based on various filters, such as vehicle type and accident year. These measures enabled the dashboard to present insights interactively, changing in real-time as users applied different filters. A key feature of the dashboard was the ability to segregate accident data by severity—fatal, serious, and slight—providing a clear visual breakdown of the impact levels.

The dashboard also featured a year-over-year (YoY) comparison, showcasing the growth or decline in accident numbers over time. Monthly accident trends were visualized to highlight specific patterns, with 2021 and 2022 data side by side for easy comparison. Slicers were added to allow users to explore accident patterns based on weather conditions, such as fine, foggy, rainy, and snowy weather, revealing how different conditions influenced the frequency of accidents.

Further analysis examined road surface conditions—dry, wet, icy, and others—and how these affected the likelihood of accidents. The dashboard also analyzed road types, comparing single carriageways, dual carriageways, and roundabouts, helping users identify high-risk road environments. Location data was visualized to contrast accident rates between urban and rural settings. Lastly, I identified the top three vehicle types (cars, motorcycles, and trucks) most frequently involved in accidents, highlighting vulnerable vehicles and providing insights for targeted safety improvements.

Conclusion

The final dashboard provided a comprehensive and interactive overview of road traffic accidents, offering valuable insights into how various factors—such as weather conditions, road surfaces, vehicle types, and accident severity—affect road safety. Users could interact with the data through slicers and filters, allowing for a deeper understanding of trends and patterns. By presenting these insights in a clear and engaging format, the dashboard made complex accident data accessible to both technical and non-technical audiences.
